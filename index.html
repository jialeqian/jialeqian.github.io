Biography

My research develops tools and models for analyzing multi-source big data to better understand individual and collective human distribution and dynamics, advancing social equity and resilience of communities and cities. Recent works focus on modeling the network among crisis evolution, government intervention, community lifeline, and public behavior in the face of public crises and societal transformations, applying deep learning-based techniques (e.g., computer vision and visual language model) to comprehend survival strategies, emergency response, and recovery dynamics of vulnerable populations across cities, and constructing a comprehensive understanding of human behavior, emotional responses, and network dynamics using multi-source big data (e.g., mobile phone location data, social media content, high-resolution satellite imagery, street view data).

I am a Visiting Scholar at the Information Sciences Institute, University of Southern California, mentored by Kristina Lerman and Keith Burghardt. Concurrently, I am in my final year as a PhD student at the State Key Laboratory of Resources and Environmental Information System, Institute of Geographic Sciences and Natural Resources Research, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the guidance of Yunyan Du. I completed my dual Bachelor's degree in Geographical Information Science and Finance with highest distinction at East China Normal University, where I collaborated with Xiang Li.

Research Interests

Unequal Urban Resilience to Rainfall

How can we accurately quantify the disparate levels of rainfall threshold and response sensitivity across cities? We examine human dynamics in response to rainfall across 346 cities employing location aware big data.【Click the image to read more】.

Human-Centric Rainstorm Standards

The rainstorm threshold of public perception is crucial for defining region-specific, human-centric rainstorm standards. Utilizing a mixed-effects model, I establish standards for 346 cities based on a dataset of over 210 million microblogs.

Community Resilience Disparity: Unraveling Disaster Dynamics

Unraveling the dynamics among disaster progression, community response, and governmental measures is crucial for tackling the disparity in social resilience. We put forth a multi-stage analytical framework and quantification techniques by innovatively considering the resistance and recovery priorities.

Bridging Linguistic Diversity Gaps in Hazard Studies

How can we address the challenge of linguistic diversity to effectively reduce biases caused by cultural, dialectical, and regional differences? I introduce a novel approach combining social media with Precipitation remote sensing observations.

Resilience of Urban Lifelines

Enhancing the evaluation of urban lifelines—essential infrastructures and services—is crucial for bolstering disaster resilience.I devise a three-tier structure for urban lifelines by integrating FEMA's lifeline criteria with word-co-occurrence patterns mined from social media data.

Mapping Homelessness: Unveiling Hidden Mechanisms

How can we achieve precise mapping of homelessness distribution and reveal its underlying mechanisms? I create a novel framework by integrating computer vision and visual language model, utilizing high-resolution satellite and street view data.

Fun Stuff

Current approaches to sports posture correction, such as basketball shooting guidance, primarily rely on individual reading comprehension or expert advice. These methods often require a significant investment of time or money and generally cannot offer personalized standards (e.g., learning Kobe Bryant's shooting form). Leveraging a vast database of sports videos, I first use human pose estimation algorithms to capture the intricate details of athletic movements, achieving high-precision 3D human pose estimation. I then compare the individual's athletic posture to personalized standard postures, integrating visual language models and physics-informed machine learning to analyze the differences and identify optimal biomechanical pathways. This provides users with an accurate and comprehensive sports posture correction plan that combines both visual and linguistic elements. Finally I develop an app for sports posture correction that not only helps users improve their athletic performance but also offers customized advice and guidance for personalized training programs and outcomes. Through precise analysis and intelligent recommendations, the app helps users better achieve their athletic goals. I have already found the colleagues from Fluid Mechanics department at UCLA, Computer Science department at USC , and the Department of Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute to collaboratively bring this exciting idea to fruition.
